Surfboard Types

Shortboard

Length: 5'6" to 6'10"

Features: Pointed nose, narrow shape, 3-5 fins

Best for: Advanced surfers, quick turns, aerial maneuvers

Longboard

Length: 8' to 12'

Features: Round nose, wide shape, single fin

Best for: Beginners, noseriding, small waves

Fish

Length: 5' to 6'6"

Features: Wide swallowtail, twin fins or quad fins (four fins)

Best for: Small waves, speed generation

Surfing Maneuvers

Wipeout

When a surfer falls off their board, often spectacularly. Common causes include losing balance, hitting sections of the wave, or misjudging maneuvers.

Barrel (Tube)

The hollow part of a breaking wave where advanced surfers ride inside. Considered the ultimate surfing experience.

Snap

A sharp, aggressive turn off the top of the wave where the surfer redirects the board downward quickly.

Carve

A smooth, powerful turn where the surfer uses the rail of the board to change direction.

Cutback

A maneuver where the surfer turns back toward the breaking part of the wave to regain positioning.

Duck Dive

Technique to push the board underwater to get past breaking waves when paddling out.

Wave Types

Beach Break

Waves breaking over sandy bottoms (example: The Wedge, California, USA)

Characteristics: Shifting peaks, can close out, generally safer for beginners

Point Break

Waves breaking along a point of land (examples: Jeffreys Bay, South Africa and Malibu, California)

Characteristics: Long, predictable rides, consistent shape

Reef Break

Waves breaking over coral or rock reef (example: Pipeline, Hawaii - best spot in the world and also the most dangerous)

Characteristics: Powerful, hollow waves, more dangerous due to shallow bottom

Surfing Etiquette

Right of Way

The surfer closest to the peak of the wave has priority. Don't drop in on someone else's wave.

Paddling Out

Paddle around the breaking waves, not through the lineup where others are surfing.

Respect Locals

Be respectful at new spots. Observe before paddling out and don't crowd the lineup.

Safety First

Always use a leash, don't ditch your board near others, and help surfers in trouble.

Surfing Gear Guide

Wetsuits

Choose thickness based on water temperature: 3/2mm for warm water, 4/3mm for cool, 5/4mm+ for cold.

Leashes

Should be about the same length as your board. Thicker for bigger waves.

Wax

Use basecoat for new boards, then apply top coat. Choose wax based on water temperature.

Fins

Different setups (single, twin, thruster, quad) affect speed, stability, and maneuverability.
